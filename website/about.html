About Safe Walk

Built because the walk home shouldn't be the most dangerous part of your night

The founder

I'm Seyedborna Boyafraz. I'm a student at TKS Toronto, and I've spent the last two years building software that solves problems I actually see around me. My last project, DebtGuard, used AI to predict financial stress before it hit. Safe Walk comes from the same instinct: take data that already exists but isn't being used, and turn it into something that helps people make better decisions.

The idea for Safe Walk came from a simple observation. I watched people I know, women especially, make the same calculation every night: is the shortcut worth the risk? They'd pull up Google Maps, see one route, and then ignore it because they knew something Google didn't. That the side street is dark. That there was an incident there last month. That the route through the park doesn't feel right after 10 PM.

That knowledge exists. It's in police databases, city infrastructure records, and the lived experience of everyone who walks these streets. But none of it shows up when you ask your phone for directions.

I'm building Safe Walk to fix that.

The mission

Safe Walk is building the world's first real-time urban safety graph. We're starting with Toronto, but the vision is bigger: a safety data layer that any navigation platform on earth can plug into.

Right now, every city has safety-relevant data sitting in disconnected silos. Police incidents in one database. Streetlight locations in another. Transit safety reports somewhere else. Community concerns in group chats and Reddit threads. No one has built the integration layer that unifies all of this into a single, scored, queryable safety graph.

That's what we're building. The consumer app is how we prove it works. The safety graph is the actual product. And every community report from every user makes it more accurate, creating a data asset that gets stronger with time.

The 10X vision

Today, a pedestrian has zero safety data when choosing a walking route. Google Maps gives you one route optimized for speed. Safe Walk gives you a route informed by thousands of data points, computed in real time: nearby incidents, lighting density, time of day, community reports.

Going from zero safety signals to thousands per route decision isn't a 10% improvement. It's going from nothing to something. That's bigger than 10X.

We want to make "safest route" as standard as "avoid tolls" and "avoid highways." It should be a default option in every navigation app, every transit planner, every campus safety system. Walking at night should stop being something you endure and become something you can plan around with actual data.

How we build

Data over assumptions

We don't guess which streets are safe. We compute it from real police records, real streetlight locations, and real community reports. When the data is limited, we say so rather than making something up.

Prevention over reaction

Most safety apps give you a panic button for when things go wrong. We route you away from danger before you encounter it. Prevention is better than response.

Infrastructure over features

We're not building a map app. We're building a safety data layer that any map app can use. The graph is the product. The consumer demo just proves it works.

Honest about trade-offs

The safest route is usually longer. We show you exactly how much longer and exactly how much safer, and let you decide. We give you information, not commands.
